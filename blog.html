Top 7 Strategies to Crack SSC CGL in Your First Attempt

The Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level exam is one of India's most competitive tests. With the right approach, you can clear it on the first try. Here's what our toppers did differently:

  • Understand the syllabus – Quant, English, GK & Reasoning. Don't skip any section.
  • Start with NCERT – Build your concepts from Class 6–12 books first.
  • Daily mock tests – Minimum 1 sectional mock per day in the final 3 months.
  • Track your weak areas – Spend 60% of study time on your weakest subject.
  • Current affairs daily – Read a newspaper or use a GK app for 30 mins every morning.
  • Speed & accuracy – Practice with a timer. 2 minutes per question is the target.
  • Revision cycles – Revise completed topics every 7 days to retain them.

Banking Exams

IBPS PO 2026: Complete Preparation Plan Month-by-Month

Banking exams are highly competitive but predictable — the pattern rarely changes. A structured 6-month plan can put you ahead of 90% of candidates. Here's our proven roadmap:

  • Month 1–2: Quantitative Aptitude basics, Number System, Simplification, Percentage.
  • Month 3: Reasoning — Puzzles, Seating Arrangements, Syllogisms, Inequalities.
  • Month 4: English — Grammar rules, Reading Comprehension, Cloze Tests, Error Spotting.
  • Month 5: General Awareness — Banking terminology, RBI policies, Current events, Static GK.
  • Month 6: Full-length mock tests daily. Analyze every paper. Target 80%+ accuracy.

Pro tip: Never ignore the interview stage. Dress formally, know about your home state's economy, and stay updated on RBI's latest policy announcements.

Punjab Police Constable: Acing Both the Written Exam & Physical Test

Punjab Police recruitment requires a dual focus — academic preparation AND physical fitness. Most candidates neglect one of the two and fail. Here's how to balance both effectively:

📚 Written Exam (100 Marks)

  • GK & Current Affairs: 40% weightage. Read Punjab-specific news daily.
  • Reasoning & Aptitude: Practice 30–40 questions every day.
  • English & Punjabi: Focus on grammar, comprehension, and vocabulary.
  • Previous year papers: Solve last 5 years' papers — pattern is very consistent.

🏃 Physical Tests

  • Male: 1600m run in under 6:30 minutes. Train daily, 3 months minimum.
  • Female: 800m run in under 4:00 minutes. Build stamina progressively.
  • Long jump & high jump: Practice technique, not just strength.
